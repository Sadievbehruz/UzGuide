<b>Zomin National Park: The "Switzerland" of Uzbekistan, a Center for Ecological Wellness, and the Sacred Treasure of the Turkestan Range</b>

<b>Zomin National Park</b>, situated within the rugged alpine periphery of the Jizzakh region and the northern slopes of the Turkestan mountain range, stands as one of the oldest and most extensive protected territories in Uzbekistan. Established in 1926, the park operates not merely as a sanctuary for rare endemic flora and fauna but as the supreme **alpine wellness and ecotourism hub** for the Central Asian region. Rising between 2,000 and 3,000 meters above sea level, the park is characterized by its alpine landscapes and soaring mountain meadows, functioning as a paramount strategic asset for the nation’s ecological sovereignty.

<b>The microclimate of the juniper forests, the physics of ionized mountain air, and ecosystem stability</b> anchor the park’s primary natural capital. The supreme asset of Zomin is its vast **Zarafshan juniper** (Archa) forests, which continuously release potent phytoncides into the atmosphere. These organic compounds effectively sterilize the air, providing high-efficacy therapeutic benefits for respiratory, nervous, and cardiovascular health. The intricate hydrological network of mountain streams and mineral springs serves as the critical regulatory backbone for maintaining the regional moisture balance of the mountain ecosystem.

<b>The topology of mountain civilization, elite mountaineering, and trekking trail systems</b> define the park’s operational potential for active tourism. The Zomin territory houses iconic landmarks such as the "Suvotkan" waterfalls, the "Sherbulak" mineral springs, and the renowned Zomin Sanatorium, which facilitate high-volume annual international visitation. The varied terrain offers diverse trajectories suitable for both professional mountaineering and leisure trekking. These zones serve as a sophisticated diplomatic and recreational platform, calibrated to international standards for sustainable mountain tourism infrastructure.

<b>Biological diversity monitoring, the preservation of "Red Book" species, and the philosophy of absolute nature protection</b> constitute the core administrative mission of the park. As a protected habitat for the Turkestan lynx, the ibex, and a variety of rare avian species, the park employs rigorous **faunal migration tracking protocols**. Functioning as a high-level scientific research center, the site serves as a crucial polygon for analyzing the impacts of global climate change on alpine ecosystems. National conservation mandates ensure the long-term, non-invasive integrity of this rare landscape for future global generations.

<b>The paradigm of the "Zomin" tourist zone project, international infrastructure integration, and ecotourism branding in New Uzbekistan</b> have transitioned the national park into a prestigious destination on the global tourism map. Under state-led initiatives, the park has been radically modernized with high-end hotel facilities, aerial ropeway systems (cable cars), and international-grade recreational complexes. These developments harmonize economic viability with the elevation of sustainable tourism culture. Today, Zomin National Park operates as a premier visual brand of New Uzbekistan’s natural wealth, ecological resilience, and global tourism competitiveness.
