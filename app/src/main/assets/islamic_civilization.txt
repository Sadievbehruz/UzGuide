<b>Center of Islamic Civilization in Uzbekistan: The Foundation of the Third Renaissance and the Spiritual Treasury of Global Science</b>

<b>The Center of Islamic Civilization in Uzbekistan</b> stands as one of the most monumental and intellectually significant enlightenment sanctuaries established globally, constructed within the historic Hazrati Imom (Khastimom) religious complex in the capital city of Tashkent. Formulated through the direct academic vision and progressive initiatives of the state leadership, this grand academy acts as a material manifestation of the historical truth that Uzbekistan served as a primary global powerhouse for Islamic culture and exact sciences for centuries. Far beyond a static museum or stationary library archive, this complex operates as a dynamic international research academy dedicated to the scientific analysis of the First and Second Eastern Renaissances, forging a vital socio-cultural bridge between our historic legacy, contemporary society, and the structural realization of the Third Renaissance.

<b>The oriental opulence of the center's architecture and advanced structural engineering</b> represent the absolute modern zenith of the national Uzbek building craft. The sheer scale and volumetric dimensions of the complex present an architectural weight rarely paralleled in contemporary urban planning. Rising at the geometric center of the compound is a colossal azure double-layered dome that systematically revives the monumental traditions of historic Bukhara and Samarkand while fully integrating advanced 21st-century engineering solutions. The external structural facades are heavily clad in premium natural stones, delicate stucco relief works, and complex tile calligraphy containing Quranic inscriptions. The interior architecture features an breathtaking layout of hand-painted traditional motifs, gilded reliefs, and structural marble columns designed to transport arriving international scholars into the golden age of medieval science.

<b>The Holy Quran of Caliph Umar and Uthman — a peerless international asset and the ideological core</b> sits prominently at the physical epicenter of the complex. The grand central rotunda explicitly showcases the absolute most sacred artifact of early Islamic history — the legendary <b>Uthman Quran</b> (Holy Mushaf of Uthman), securely preserved within a specialized high-tech environmental capsule designed for permanent conservation. Encircling this holy relic are expansive multi-thematic museum halls illustrating the true peaceful, humanistic nature of Islamic theology. The multi-tiered museum contains numerous scientific wings, each systematically mapping out specific historic eras, highlighting the exact academic breakthroughs achieved by regional polymaths in the fields of astronomy, mathematics, medicine, chemistry, geodesy, and classical philosophy.

<b>The rare manuscript repository and global digital library networks</b> open unprecedented horizons for international orientalists and specialized researchers. The integrated research library holds an expansive physical and digital asset base exceeding 100,000 rare medieval manuscripts, early lithographed books, and historical state decrees. Through international diplomatic partnerships, the center has systematically consolidated a centralized database containing digital facsimiles of foundational treatises written by Avicenna, Al-Khwarizmi, Biruni, Ulugh Beg, and Imam al-Bukhari, whose original copies reside in London, Paris, Berlin, and Cairo. A state-of-the-art chemical restoration laboratory operates continuously within the complex to clean, repair, and digitally preserve degrading paper fibers utilizing advanced molecular techniques.

<b>The International Research Academy and global academic synchronization</b> guarantee the active strategic positioning of the institution within the global scientific community. The facility functions as a prime international forum connecting elite Islamic scholars, textologists, archeologists, and social scientists from across the globe. The institution has solidified binding structural partnerships with UNESCO, ISESCO, IRCICA, and elite international universities, facilitating the execution of periodic international symposiums, cross-border archeological projects, and high-level scientific exhibitions. A primary mission of the academy is the counter-ideological defense of Islamic thought against radical distortion, utilizing definitive historical data to demonstrate the peaceful, enlightened, and intellectually progressive nature of the faith.

<b>In terms of cultural capital, strategic value, and future civilizational output</b>, the Center of Islamic Civilization remains the premier intellectual brand of New Uzbekistan on the global stage. The complex has risen to the status of an absolute primary state landmark, regularly hosting visiting heads of state, foreign diplomatic corps, and millions of international cultural tourists. It stands as an unshakeable manifest of the Uzbek people's profound reverence for their intellectual ancestry, national identity, and commitment to global academic progress. In conclusion, the Center of Islamic Civilization in Uzbekistan remains an eternal lighthouse of knowledge, channeling the brilliant intellectual rays of our ancient history into the modern world to illuminate the paths of future global generations.
